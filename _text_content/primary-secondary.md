## [EXPOSITION]
**Mixing primary and secondary colors**

## [EXPOSITION]
Following the lesson, using color cloning, you will create the secondary colors **Cyan**, **Magenta**, and **Yellow** from the primary colors.

## [EXPOSITION]
1. Color the top row shapes the primary colors: **Red**, **Green**, **Blue**

<!-- SHAPE_TRIGGERS: [{"shape": 0, "color": "#ff0000"}, {"shape": 1, "color": "#00ff00"}, {"shape": 2, "color": "#0000ff"}] -->
<!-- TRIGGER_SUCCESS: Primary colors locked in — Red, Green, Blue. -->


## [EXPOSITION]
2. Select the Red shape, hold down the **Shift** key and click on the shape below it to make it **Red**.

3. Click on the shape again to select just it.

## [EXPOSITION]
4. Use the colormixer interface to add **Green** to the **Red** shape. You now have **Yellow** — a secondary color, being the mix of two primary channel colors, **Red** and **Green**.

<!-- SHAPE_TRIGGERS: [{"shape": 3, "color": "#ffff00"}] -->
<!-- TRIGGER_SUCCESS: You've got it! Yellow is Red + Green. -->

## [EXPOSITION]
5. Repeat this process with **Green**, adding **Blue** to create **Cyan**.

<!-- SHAPE_TRIGGERS: [{"shape": 4, "color": "#00ffff"}] -->
<!-- TRIGGER_SUCCESS: You've got it! Cyan is Green + Blue. -->

## [EXPOSITION]
6. Finally, clone **Blue**, adding **Red** to create **Magenta**.

<!-- SHAPE_TRIGGERS: [{"shape": 5, "color": "#ff00ff"}] -->
<!-- TRIGGER_SUCCESS: You've got it! Magenta is Blue + Red. -->

## [EXPOSITION]
Now, you can see how secondary colors are derived from mixing two primary colors.
